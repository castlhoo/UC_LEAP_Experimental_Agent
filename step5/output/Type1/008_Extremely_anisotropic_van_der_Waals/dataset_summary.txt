- Raman spectroscopy / Raman thermometry
  - Used to track temperature-dependent Raman peak shifts under laser heating
- Molecular dynamics (MD) simulations
  - Used to compute in-plane and out-of-plane thermal conductivity and compare with experiment
- Electrical transport / device characterization setup

- Likely microfabricated heater/electrode structures

b. Experimental Context (Inference)
- Likely a thermal transport study of van der Waals materials with strong anisotropy
- Main phenomena inferred:
  - Through-plane thermal resistance / thermal boundary resistance
  - In-plane vs out-of-plane thermal conductivity anisotropy
  - Laser-heating-induced Raman peak shifts for thermometry
  - Device-level heat spreading and thermal insulation behavior
  - Electrical robustness / current-carrying behavior in the presence of r-MoS2
- Reasoning:
  - TDTR traces and `R_TDTR` strongly indicate thermal boundary/transport measurements
  - MD simulation columns for `k_in-plane` and `k_out-of-plane` indicate atomistic thermal conductivity modeling
  - Raman spectra vs absorbed power and peak shift fits are characteristic of Raman thermometry
  - Temperature maps and I-V curves suggest device thermal management and electrical characterization

c. Notes for Analysis
- The dataset is organized by figure panels rather than by run IDs or experimental metadata
- Many sheets contain paired experimental and fitted/model columns, which is useful for validation but requires careful column grouping
- Some sheets mix multiple subplots/insets in one table, especially Fig. 3b and Fig. 4
- Potential ambiguities:
  - `N` likely denotes layer number, but this is not explicitly defined in the tables
  - `r-MoS2` and `r-WS2` are inferred material labels; the exact meaning of `r` is not stated in the tables themselves
  - Units are embedded in headers and should be parsed carefully
- Suggested analysis approaches:
  - TDTR: fit time-domain traces to extract thermal parameters and compare across `N`
  - Thermal anisotropy: compare in-plane vs out-of-plane conductivity trends vs temperature
  - Raman thermometry: fit peak shift vs absorbed power to estimate local thermal conductance
  - Device thermal maps: compare bare vs r-MoS2-covered structures for heat spreading effects
  - Electrical curves: compare I-V behavior and critical current changes between conditions
